Chapter 1 - What is BNF?

BNF (Backus-Naur Form) is a type of documentation that should explain the grammar of your language. It essentially describes how the interpreter should interpret your language. It should be said that BNF isn't a strict syntax or anything. There are many variants and the rules are loose in general. As long as you can make yourself understood, you'll get far. All throughout this course, I will use the dialect I'm used to.

I'd say that the simplest way to explain BNF is through examples. Otherwise it might get very abstract and confusing.
